Problem Number: 001

Problem Title: Multiples of 3 or 5

Problem Definition: If we list all the natural numbers below 10 that are multiples of 3 or 5, we get 3, 5, 6, and 9; The sum of these multiples is 23.

Problem Question: Find the sum of all the multiples of 3 or 5 below 1000.

That begs the question: Isn't there a more elegant solution?

And indeed there is one.

The Arithmetic Progression Series allows us to compute it neatly!

$$ S_n = \frac{n}{2} [2a + (n-1)d] $$
given a is the first term, d is the common difference, and n is the number of terms in the sum, we can find the sum of multiples of any number.

Since we want to sum multiples, d will be equal to a, and n will be the rounded down integer of the limit divided by the multiple.

We will have to do this for both multiples, and deduct the repeated terms (ie. the sum of the lowest common multiplier's Arithmetic Progression).
